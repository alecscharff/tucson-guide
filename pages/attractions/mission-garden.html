Mission Garden

Living Agricultural History

Kid Appeal: ★★☆☆☆

Overview

A living agricultural museum recreating the history of farming in the Tucson basin over the last 4,000 years. Located at the foot of Sentinel Peak ("A" Mountain).

Highlights

  • Heritage Crops: Fruit trees, crops, and medicinal plants from different eras (O'odham, Spanish, Mexican, Chinese, Territorial).
  • Chickens: Always a hit with kids.
  • Acequias: Historic irrigation canals.
  • Events: Frequent tastings and educational events.

Essential Information

Address: 946 W Mission Ln, Tucson, AZ 85745

Cost: Donation suggested ($5/person)

Time Needed: 45 mins - 1 hour

For Kids

It's an educational stop where they can see where food comes from. There isn't a playground, but there are chickens and open space. Best for older kids interested in history or nature.
